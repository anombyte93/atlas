# Chemical Risks Reference

This reference lists common food additives and preservatives that are frequently flagged for potential health concerns. It is for educational screening, not medical advice.

## Legend
- Category: preservative, dye, sweetener, antioxidant, emulsifier, stabilizer, flavor enhancer, acidulant
- Typical concerns: allergy risk, behavioral sensitivity, gut irritation, carcinogenic concern, metabolic effects

## Chemicals and Potential Risks

### Sodium nitrite (E250)
- Category: preservative
- Typical concerns: forms nitrosamines in certain conditions; linked to processed meat risk in epidemiology
- Common in: bacon, hot dogs, deli meats

### Sodium benzoate (E211)
- Category: preservative
- Typical concerns: can form benzene with vitamin C under heat/light; may trigger sensitivity in some people
- Common in: soft drinks, sauces

### Potassium sorbate / Sorbic acid (E202/E200)
- Category: preservative
- Typical concerns: skin and mucosal irritation in sensitive individuals
- Common in: baked goods, cheese products

### TBHQ (E319)
- Category: antioxidant
- Typical concerns: high-dose animal studies show adverse effects; may trigger nausea or hyperactivity in sensitive people
- Common in: instant noodles, snack foods

### BHA (E320)
- Category: antioxidant
- Typical concerns: classified as possibly carcinogenic in animal studies
- Common in: snack foods, cereals

### BHT (E321)
- Category: antioxidant
- Typical concerns: mixed evidence; potential endocrine and liver effects in animal studies
- Common in: cereals, snack foods

### Artificial colors (Red 40 E129, Yellow 5 E102, Yellow 6 E110, Blue 1 E133)
- Category: dye
- Typical concerns: behavioral sensitivity in some children; possible allergy-like reactions
- Common in: candies, cereals, drinks

### Caramel color (E150a-d)
- Category: color
- Typical concerns: some types can contain 4-MEI byproduct; potential cancer concern at high intake
- Common in: colas, sauces

### Aspartame (E951)
- Category: sweetener
- Typical concerns: not recommended for people with PKU; headaches reported by some users
- Common in: diet sodas, sugar-free products

### Sucralose (E955)
- Category: sweetener
- Typical concerns: may alter gut microbiome in some studies; heat can form chlorinated byproducts
- Common in: flavored waters, protein products

### Monosodium glutamate (E621)
- Category: flavor enhancer
- Typical concerns: sensitivity reactions reported (headache, flushing) in some individuals
- Common in: soups, snacks, seasoning packets

### Disodium inosinate / disodium guanylate (E631/E627)
- Category: flavor enhancer
- Typical concerns: can trigger reactions in sensitive individuals; often used with MSG
- Common in: chips, ramen, sauces

### Phosphates (E339)
- Category: acidity regulator
- Typical concerns: high intake linked to vascular and kidney strain; may impact bone health
- Common in: processed meats, frozen items

### Carrageenan (E407)
- Category: stabilizer
- Typical concerns: possible gut irritation; controversial in animal and in-vitro studies
- Common in: dairy, plant milks

### Calcium disodium EDTA (E385)
- Category: preservative
- Typical concerns: may bind minerals; generally safe at low doses but flagged by some as undesirable
- Common in: dressings, sauces

### Propionates (Calcium propionate E282)
- Category: preservative
- Typical concerns: may cause irritability or headaches in sensitive individuals
- Common in: breads, tortillas

### Xanthan gum (E415)
- Category: stabilizer
- Typical concerns: GI discomfort in some people at higher intakes
- Common in: dressings, sauces

### Gellan gum (E418)
- Category: stabilizer
- Typical concerns: mild GI effects in sensitive individuals
- Common in: plant milks

### Maltodextrin
- Category: thickener
- Typical concerns: high glycemic index; may affect gut microbiome in some studies
- Common in: packaged sauces, snacks
