## Top 20 Additives to Avoid

### HIGH RISK ADDITIVES (Red - Avoid)

#### 1. Potassium Bromate (E924)
- **Type**: Flour treatment agent/oxidizing agent
- **Common Uses**: Bread, rolls, buns (helps dough rise)
- **Health Risks**: Kidney and thyroid cancers in rodents
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Banned
  - US: Permitted (California banned in 2023)
  - China, India, Brazil, Canada: Banned
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: EFSA evaluations, California Proposition 65 listing

#### 2. Brominated Vegetable Oil (BVO)
- **Type**: Emulsifier
- **Common Uses**: Citrus-flavored beverages
- **Health Risks**: Bromine accumulation, neurological concerns
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Banned
  - US: Permitted but being phased out by many manufacturers
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: FDA, EU food additive regulations

#### 3. Red 3 (Erythrosine, E127)
- **Type**: Artificial color
- **Common Uses**: Candies, baked goods, maraschino cherries
- **Health Risks**: Thyroid tumors in animal studies
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Banned for food use
  - US: FDA revoked authorization in 2025
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: FDA final determination, EFSA

#### 4. Partially Hydrogenated Oils (Trans Fats)
- **Type**: Fat source/emulsifier
- **Common Uses**: Processed foods, baked goods, fried foods
- **Health Risks**:
  - Increases LDL ("bad") cholesterol
  - Increases coronary heart disease risk more than any other macronutrient
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Restricted
  - US: FDA determined not GRAS in 2015, final ban effective December 2023
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: [FDA Trans Fat](https://www.fda.gov/food/food-additives-petitions/trans-fat), [NEJM](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMp1314072)

#### 5. Sodium/Potassium Benzoate (E211, E212)
- **Type**: Preservative
- **Common Uses**: Soft drinks, fruit juices, pickles, condiments
- **Health Risks**:
  - Benzene formation (known carcinogen) when combined with Vitamin C
  - Triggered by heat and light exposure
  - Potential links to asthma, allergies, ADHD
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Permitted with restrictions
  - US: GRAS status
- **Risk Level**: HIGH (due to benzene formation potential)
- **Sources**: [FDA Benzene Q&A](https://www.fda.gov/food/environmental-contaminants-food/questions-and-answers-occurrence-benzene-soft-drinks-and-other-beverages), [Healthline](https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/sodium-benzoate)

#### 6. Nitrites/Nitrates (E249, E250, E251, E252)
- **Type**: Preservative (curing agent)
- **Common Uses**: Processed meats (bacon, ham, hot dogs, sausages)
- **Health Risks**:
  - Form carcinogenic nitrosamines during processing/cooking
  - Processed meat classified as Group 1 carcinogen by WHO/IARC
  - Linked to colorectal cancer
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Permitted with strict limits
  - US: Permitted
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: [WHO Q&A](https://www.who.int/news-room/questions-and-answers/item/cancer-carcinogenicity-of-the-consumption-of-red-meat-and-processed-meat), [EFSA](https://www.efsa.europa.eu/sites/default/files/corporate_publications/files/nitrates-nitrites-170614.pdf)

#### 7. BHA (Butylated Hydroxyanisole, E320)
- **Type**: Preservative/antioxidant
- **Common Uses**: Cereals, chewing gum, snacks, packaged foods
- **Health Risks**: Potential carcinogen, endocrine disruptor concerns
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Severely restricted
  - US: Permitted (GRAS)
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: CSPI Chemical Cuisine, EFSA evaluations

#### 8. BHT (Butylated Hydroxytoluene, E321)
- **Type**: Preservative/antioxidant
- **Common Uses**: Cereals, snacks, packaged foods
- **Health Risks**: Potential carcinogen, organ system toxicity
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Severely restricted
  - US: Permitted (GRAS)
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: CSPI Chemical Cuisine, EFSA evaluations

#### 9. Propylparaben
- **Type**: Preservative
- **Common Uses**: Processed foods, beverages
- **Health Risks**:
  - Endocrine disruptor (mimics estrogen)
  - Alters hormone function
  - Listed on EU potential Category 1 endocrine disruptors list
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Not banned but under scrutiny as potential endocrine disruptor
  - US: Permitted
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: [EU SCCS Opinion](https://health.ec.europa.eu/system/files/2022-08/sccs_o_243.pdf), [EWG](https://www.ewg.org/research/propyl-paraben)

#### 10. Titanium Dioxide (E171)
- **Type**: White pigment/color additive
- **Common Uses**: Candies, baked goods, pastries, pharmaceuticals
- **Health Risks**:
  - Genotoxicity concerns (DNA damage potential)
  - Intestinal inflammation
  - Inhalation risks
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Banned since August 2022
  - US: Permitted
  - UK: Concluded unlikely to pose health risks at current exposure levels
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: EFSA 2021 opinion reversing 2016 safety assessment

### MEDIUM RISK ADDITIVES (Yellow - Caution)

#### 11. Red 40 (Allura Red AC, E129)
- **Type**: Artificial color
- **Common Uses**: Beverages, candies, baked goods, cereals
- **Health Risks**:
  - Hyperactivity and attention deficits in children
  - Hypersensitivity reactions (hives, swelling)
  - One of the most common allergens among artificial dyes
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Permitted with warning label ("may have an adverse effect on activity and attention in children")
  - US: Permitted
- **Risk Level**: MEDIUM
- **Sources**: [CSPI Rainbow of Risks](https://www.cspi.org/sites/default/files/attachment/food-dyes-rainbow-of-risks.pdf), [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23026007/)

#### 12. Yellow 5 (Tartrazine, E102)
- **Type**: Artificial color
- **Common Uses**: Candies, beverages, baked goods, cereals
- **Health Risks**:
  - Hyperactivity in children
  - Allergic reactions, especially in aspirin-sensitive individuals
  - One of the most common allergens among artificial dyes
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Permitted with warning label
  - US: Permitted (must be declared by name)
- **Risk Level**: MEDIUM
- **Sources**: CSPI, PubMed toxicology studies

#### 13. Yellow 6 (Sunset Yellow FCF, E110)
- **Type**: Artificial color
- **Common Uses**: Candies, baked goods, cereals, beverages
- **Health Risks**:
  - Hyperactivity in children
  - Hypersensitivity reactions
  - Potential adrenal gland effects in animal studies
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Permitted with warning label
  - US: Permitted
- **Risk Level**: MEDIUM
- **Sources**: CSPI Rainbow of Risks, PubMed studies

#### 14. Blue 1 (Brilliant Blue FCF, E133)
- **Type**: Artificial color
- **Common Uses**: Beverages, candies, baked goods, ice cream
- **Health Risks**:
  - Hypersensitivity reactions (hives, swelling)
  - Potential allergic responses in sensitive individuals
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - EU: Permitted
  - US: Permitted
- **Risk Level**: MEDIUM
- **Sources**: CSPI, PubMed toxicology studies

#### 15. Aspartame (E951)
- **Type**: Artificial sweetener
- **Common Uses**: Diet beverages, sugar-free products, tabletop sweeteners
- **Health Risks**:
  - WHO/IARC classified as "possibly carcinogenic to humans" (Group 2B, 2023)
  - Linked to cardiovascular concerns (stroke, cerebrovascular events)
  - Controversial weight gain effects
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - FDA: GRAS (50 mg/kg body weight/day)
  - EU: Permitted
  - WHO: Group 2B carcinogen classification
- **Risk Level**: MEDIUM
- **Sources**: [WHO Assessment](https://www.who.int/news/item/14-07-2023-aspartame-hazard-and-risk-assessment-results-released), [FDA](https://www.fda.gov/food/food-additives-petitions/aspartame-and-other-sweeteners-food)

#### 16. Sucralose (E955)
- **Type**: Artificial sweetener
- **Common Uses**: Diet beverages, sugar-free products, baked goods
- **Health Risks**:
  - Associated with increased cardiovascular disease risk
  - Potential gut microbiome disruption
  - Controversial metabolic effects
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - FDA: Permitted (5 mg/kg body weight/day)
  - EU: Permitted
- **Risk Level**: MEDIUM
- **Sources**: [PMC Health Impact Study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10822749/), [Harvard Health](https://www.health.harvard.edu/heart-health/sugar-substitutes-new-cardiovascular-concerns)

#### 17. Acesulfame Potassium / Ace-K (E950)
- **Type**: Artificial sweetener
- **Common Uses**: Diet beverages, sugar-free products (often combined with aspartame)
- **Health Risks**:
  - Associated with increased cardiovascular disease risk
  - Potential metabolic disruption
  - Ongoing safety debates
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - FDA: Permitted (15 mg/kg body weight/day)
  - EU: Permitted
- **Risk Level**: MEDIUM
- **Sources**: PMC studies, Harvard Health

### LOW/MEDIUM RISK ADDITIVES (Green/Yellow - Limited)

#### 18. Monosodium Glutamate (MSG, E621)
- **Type**: Flavor enhancer
- **Common Uses**: Processed foods, snacks, restaurant meals, Asian cuisine
- **Health Risks**:
  - Short-term, mild symptoms in some individuals (headache, numbness)
  - "Chinese Restaurant Syndrome" largely debunked by research
  - Overwhelming scientific consensus of safety
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - FDA: GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe)
  - EU: Authorized food additive
  - WHO/FAO JECFA: Established ADI, confirmed safe
- **Risk Level**: LOW
- **Sources**: [WHO/FAO JECFA Database](https://www.who.int/teams/food-nutrition/jecfa), extensive research studies

#### 19. Carrageenan (E407)
- **Type**: Thickener/stabilizer
- **Common Uses**: Dairy products, plant milks, processed meats
- **Health Risks**:
  - Potential digestive inflammation (controversial)
  - Degraded carrageenan (poligeenan) is not approved for food use
  - Food-grade carrageenan generally recognized as safe
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - FDA: GRAS
  - EU: Permitted
  - JECFA: Established ADI
- **Risk Level**: LOW-MEDIUM
- **Sources**: CSPI notes uncertainty, but regulatory bodies maintain safety

#### 20. Caramel Colors (E150a-d)
- **Type**: Color additive
- **Common Uses**: Colas, baked goods, sauces, beer
- **Health Risks**:
  - E150c (ammonia process) contains 4-MEI, potential carcinogen
  - E150d (ammonia sulfite process) similar concerns
  - E150a and E150b considered safer
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - FDA: Permitted
  - EU: Permitted with 4-MEI limits for E150c and E150d
  - California: Listed under Proposition 65 for 4-MEI
- **Risk Level**: LOW-MEDIUM (varies by type)
- **Sources**: CSPI, California Proposition 65, EFSA

## Regulatory Comparison: EU vs US Approach

### Fundamental Difference

- **EU**: Precautionary principle - additives banned until proven safe
- **US**: Additives allowed until proven harmful

### Additives Banned in EU, Allowed in US

1. Potassium Bromate (E924)
2. Brominated Vegetable Oil (BVO)
3. Red 3 (E127) - FDA revoked authorization in 2025
4. BHA (E320) - severely restricted in EU
5. BHT (E321) - severely restricted in EU
6. Titanium Dioxide (E171)

### Recent US Regulatory Actions (2025)

Four additives banned in California and under federal scrutiny:
- Potassium Bromate
- Propylparaben
- Brominated Vegetable Oil
- Red Dye No. 3

## Conclusions and Recommendations

### Key Findings

1. **Regulatory Divergence**: EU takes more precautionary approach, banning additives still permitted in US
2. **Vulnerable Populations**: Children particularly susceptible to artificial colors (hyperactivity effects)
3. **Carcinogenic Concerns**: Multiple additives linked to cancer in animal studies or classified by WHO/IARC
4. **Emerging Research**: Cardiovascular risks of artificial sweeteners under active investigation

### Molly Food Scanner Implementation

1. **High-Priority Additives**: Focus on EU-banned substances for red warnings
2. **Artificial Colors**: All require yellow warnings due to behavioral effects in children
3. **Preservatives**: Nitrites/nitrates and benzoates warrant high warnings due to carcinogenic potential
4. **Sweeteners**: Mixed risk profile - aspartame (yellow), sucralose/ace-K (yellow-green boundary)
5. **Consumer Education**: Provide clear explanations of risks with authoritative source citations
